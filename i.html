I

During this project, I would like to become a better listener. I want to learn to manage my enthusiasm, avoid interrupting others, and give people the space to speak fully before I respond. I can get carried away, especially when I’m excited about a topic. These interruptions can disrupt conversations and affect collaboration. The article Interruptions and Silences in Conversations: A Turn-Taking Analysis supports my reflections. It shows how interruptions, even when well-intentioned, can disturb the flow of conversation or unintentionally exclude others (Lestary et al., 2018). It also reminds me that silence has meaning — it creates space for thought and emotional signals, which I sometimes rush past in my eagerness to contribute.

This is important to me because I believe this habit stands in the way of truly expressing myself and listening to others. I’m at a stage in my life and career where I expect more of myself. I’ve been working on this issue already, but I would like to use this project as the final push to overcome it.

In the Way Forward section, I propose ideas to support this change.

Reference

Lestary, R., Kamimoto, T., & Yamada, M. (2018). Interruptions and silences in conversations: A turn-taking analysis. Retrieved from https://www.researchgate.net/publication/328660344_Interruptions_and_Silences_in_Conversations_A_Turn-Taking_Analysis
