Obstacles

Going back to university brings a few challenges I didn’t fully expect. One of them is working in a team where almost everyone is from a younger generation. At Advier, I’m usually not the oldest person in the room. There, I work alongside seniors and have guidance from more experienced colleagues. Here, I’m often the oldest — and in many cases, the only one with professional work experience. That changes the group dynamic.

Another challenge is language. I’ve been working almost entirely in Dutch for the past few years. Although I speak English fluently, I’m less experienced in using formal academic English. This affects how confident I feel when speaking in class or writing for university.

Lastly, this is my first time at a research university. I did my bachelor’s at the Hogeschool van Amsterdam, a university of applied sciences. That gave me a solid foundation, but the academic expectations here are different and sometimes unfamiliar.

All of these things stand in the way of me being the best student I can be. They influence how I work, how I communicate, and how much I feel I belong in this environment.
