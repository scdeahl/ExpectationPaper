Obstacles

On my journey to the top of the mountain — toward the project outcome (It) and my long-term goals — I face both personal and academic challenges.

Firstly, there are personal obstacles that affect me and my team. I tend to interrupt when I get excited, which can disrupt conversations. I sometimes take over tasks or try to control outcomes because I struggle to fully trust others’ pace, quality, or methods. Perfectionism and a strong drive to achieve can also get in the way of genuine collaboration. These habits, though well-intentioned, can make it harder for others to contribute and for the group to find its own rhythm.

Secondly, there are obstacles in my academic journey. One of them is my formal English. During my modelling career, I became fluent in English, but not in an academic or professional context. At Advier, we mainly communicate in Dutch, so I haven’t had much real-world practice with formal English. This sometimes makes me feel less confident when presenting or speaking in class, even when I know what I want to say.
