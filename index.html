Expectation 'paper'

Welcome to this website. Why a website instead of a paper? There are a few reasons:

First, I wanted to visualise my personal learning journey. I was inspired by the GROW and I-WE-IT frameworks and aimed to combine them. I chose to show the steps in the order of Reality, Obstacles, I, Way Forward, We, It, and Goals because it reflects how I move through a learning process—from understanding my current situation, facing challenges, and reflecting on myself, to planning actions and involving others, before aiming for impact and long-term goals. To support this, I created a mountain illustration in Photoshop. It helps to visualise the journey: where I begin, what I encounter along the way, and what I am working towards. By clicking on the steps or words, you can access further explanations.

Second, the website allows for direct integration of a feedback and progress-tracking system. In the bottom left corner, there is a button called ‘Help me track progress’. Here, my teammates from the Expert Team and the MT Team—but possibly also others—can give me feedback throughout the project. In the coming week, I will connect this to a results section, where progress can be tracked live. I plan to present this to my teammates during our Monday meeting and will also share it with the MT Team.

Finally, I saw this as an opportunity to apply and refresh my web development skills. I used HTML and Visual Studio Code to build the site, with Live Preview for real-time updates. I used ChatGPT to improve the code and work more efficiently. The website is hosted via GitHub, which also serves as its version control platform.

I hope this provides a clear and engaging overview of my learning journey.
